Many people, myself included want to take notes while reading a book, usually for referencing at a later point in time. Apart from this reminciscent nature of the notes, they also serve the purpose of keeping track of things that you want to check later, like other books' names. It is very clear that we are heading towards a future where we won't be using printed books as much as we have until this time. Among other readers, Kindle is a very convenient way of reading digital books. The problem is that the Kindle doesn't play well with note taking. It has the functionality to take notes and even share them on social networks but currently there is no easy way of using those notes later. The 'MyClippings.txt' file created on your Kindle after you start taking notes is close to useless. If you share your notes on social networks your notes are also stored in your kindle.amazon.com account but these notes are scattered and offer you very little flexibility in organizing them.

Enter KindleNotes. With an application that works on Twitter you can now have all the flexibility with your notes on your Kindle. Here's a list of all the futures the KindleNotes app will have: * All your notes from your reading history organized in an easy to understand way. You can even change the organizing criteria! * Never worry about making sense of your 'MyClippings.txt' file again. You have the option of uploading your 'MyClippings.txt' file and we will import all your notes to your account. * Easy to create notes that will magically appear on your account. Just send a tweet to @kindlenotes when sharing on Twitter and we will create the note on your account. * Unlimited book notes subscribers. Free users will have the option of creating notes for only one book at a time. * Reading more than one book at a time? Define special keywords for your books from your account settings and include this keyword in your tweet and we will append the note to the appropriate book in your bookshelf. Now you can take notes on your Kindle and not worry about those notes later on. You can always export all your notes should you decide not to use KindleNotes again.

FAQ

What will you use the funds for?
The funds will be used for software development and acquisition of testing devices. While Kindle Notes will debut for the Kindle only, we plan on adding support for a wide array of other readers in the future.
Will I be able to make notes public or private as I want?
Yes! You will be able to set global preferences (like 'never make any of notes public' and those notes will be stored as private notes until you decide to take them public. You will also have the ability to set these options on a per book basis. For example, you might set up your account such that the notes from the first book you're reading to be private by default, and those from the second book you're reading to be public by default.
